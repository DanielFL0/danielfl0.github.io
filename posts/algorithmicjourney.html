A deep journey into algorithms

Too much time spent talking with my software engineer friends made me realize I suck big time at algorithms, I know exactly the reason why, I haven't practiced enough and it's time to change that.
These days most of the content I consume is related to competitive programming, I particularly enjoy watching Errichto's youtube videos, as well as William Lin's youtube videos, my current goal is to get 1400+ in codeforces until the end of 2022.
I've yet to come with a full list of topics I will be learning to make this possible, I'm thinking I will either need to be learning some Java or C++ as these seem to be popular tools in the competitive programming space.

Is 1400+ my end goal?

No. Not really, at first glance I just assumed it's a feasible achivement given my current circumstances, but 1400+ is just a number after all, what I want to get from this challenge is the ability to solve problems faster and get a general understanding of the different techniques and approaches that are used in problem solving, I strongly believe this will help me improve as a programmer.
